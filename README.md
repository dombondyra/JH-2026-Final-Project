# Data Patterns And Representations Final Project

## Project Topic
Introductory paragraph

Our proposed topic examines how individuals and society can most effectively reduce their carbon footprint, by separating the actions that meaningfully cut greenhouse gas emissions from those that feel impactful but barely move the needle. We plan to start by breaking emissions by sector to see where the largest sources actually are, then work through those sources to identify which changes matter most at both the individual and policy level. 

Importance

In recent years, global warming has become a significant topic of concern among scientists and the population at large. There have been many proposed efforts to lower greenhouse gas emissions and decrease society’s carbon footprint, and in this project we aim to analyze just how effective each effort is. We only have one Earth and we should all learn how to better take care of it and encourage others to do so as well. 
The topic is personally and professionally relevant: a large share of the food received at food pantries comes in thanks to California’s SB 1383. Under this law, food generators and distributors such as supermarkets and grocery stores are mandated to donate their surplus edible food to food recovery organizations such as food banks and pantries rather than sending it to the landfill, aiming to rescue at least 20% of surplus edible food. 

Following is a list of questions that we would like to explore in this project:
- How do total emissions break down by sector? 
- How much better are electric vehicles for the environment than gas powered vehicles?
- What types of foods reduce carbon footprint? How do common foods compare in emissions per serving or per gram of protein - for example, beef and lamb vs chicken, and plant-based proteins? How much could one person cut their footprint by shifting their diet? 
- What types of transportation reduce carbon footprint? How does each mode compare in emissions per passenger-mile? 
- Are there any other behaviors that reduce carbon footprint significantly?
- What types of changes can be made in policy vs on an individual level to decrease carbon footprints?
- Which single behaviors produce the biggest reductions? Car-free, skipping a long flight, switching to renewable home energy, or changing diet? 
- Tension between individual responsibility vs large industrial or systemic sources?
